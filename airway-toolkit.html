Pre-Oxygenation Standardization
Priority-based pre-ox hierarchy by clinical scenario
▼
1
BiPAP / CPAP
▸ CHF · COPD · Cooperative patient
First Line — PerIOx Trial
Target SpO₂ ≥95% before laryngoscopy
Servo U (Rooms 15, 16, 27) — BiPAP + HFNC capable
If agitated → DSI first, then BiPAP
2
High-Flow Nasal Cannula
▸ BiPAP not tolerated · Apneic oxygenation during attempts
Second Line
40–60 L/min adults; leave in place during laryngoscopy
Run concurrently with BiPAP if tolerated
Servo U supports HFNC mode
3
NRB + Nasal Cannula
▸ NIPPV not tolerated · Obtunded but breathing
Alternative
NRB at 15 L/min + NC at max flow (15 L/min)
Consider DSI (ketamine) → then place BiPAP
NC remains in place during intubation
4
BVM + NC + PEEP Valve
▸ Peri-arrest · Apneic · Pre-arrest respiratory failure
Rescue / Bridge
NC at max flow (15 L/min) flush under mask
PEEP valve 5–10 cmH₂O on BVM
Two-person BVM technique preferred
Bridge to BiPAP or intubation as rapidly as possible
💉
Delayed Sequence Intubation (DSI)
Ketamine-assisted pre-oxygenation for agitated patients
▼
⚠ Use when agitation or combativeness prevents adequate pre-oxygenation

1
Administer Ketamine
1–2 mg/kg IV or 4–5 mg/kg IM — dissociative dose
Patient maintains airway reflexes and respiratory drive
2
Place BiPAP or HFNC
Now cooperative — apply preferred pre-ox modality
Allow 3–5 minutes of optimization
3
Confirm SpO₂ Optimized
Target ≥95% before proceeding
4
Proceed with Standard RSI
Succinylcholine 1.5 mg/kg or Rocuronium 1.2 mg/kg IV push
Paralytic as per standard protocol
💡 Ketamine given during DSI counts toward your induction agent — adjust accordingly

Aintree Catheter Walkthrough
Intubation through iGel via Aintree + fiberoptic
▼
Use after 3 min DeNitro with Good SpO₂ via iGel — or when direct laryngoscopy has failed ×3

1
Place iGel (if not already in)
Size by weight — confirm ventilation and ETCO₂
Secure in place; do not advance ET tube yet
2
Load Aintree Catheter onto Bronchoscope
Thread Aintree catheter over the flexible bronchoscope (purple B-Flex glidescope bronchoscope fits)
Catheter is 2× length of ET tube — prevents loss of introducer
3
Advance Bronchoscope Through iGel into Trachea
Navigate scope through iGel lumen → glottis → trachea
Confirm carina and tracheal rings on scope view
4
Advance Aintree Catheter; Remove Scope
Hold Aintree in position — withdraw bronchoscope while keeping catheter in trachea
5
Remove iGel
Carefully remove supraglottic airway while maintaining Aintree position in trachea
Do not lose the Aintree catheter!
6
Thread ET Tube Over Aintree
Size 6.0 ET tube minimum (limited by iGel inner diameter)
Advance ET tube over catheter into trachea; remove Aintree
7
Confirm Placement
Continuous waveform capnography · Bilateral breath sounds · CXR when stabilized
⚠ Training: Practice with airway mannequins + iGel devices in airway closet before clinical use
🩸
Needle Jet Insufflation
Cannot intubate / Cannot oxygenate rescue — last resort
▼
🚨 CICO Situation — Use only when cricothyrotomy not immediately possible or as bridge

1
Identify Cricothyroid Membrane
Extend neck · Palpate thyroid cartilage → inferior notch → CTM soft spot
Use ultrasound if landmark unclear
2
Insert 14g Angiocath
45° caudal angle through CTM · Aspirate air to confirm tracheal position
Remove needle — advance catheter only
3
Attach High-Pressure Oxygen Source
Y-connector or 3mL syringe barrel + 15mm ET tube adapter
Wall O₂ at 15 L/min or jet ventilator if available
4
Ventilate: 1 sec IN / 4 sec OUT
Occlude Y-port 1 second → release 4 seconds
Watch for chest rise; allow full passive exhalation
5
Proceed to Definitive Surgical Airway
Needle jet is a bridge — target <45 min before hypercarbia · Escalate to cricothyrotomy or call thoracic surgery immediately
⚠ Risk of barotrauma if expiration obstructed. Ensure upper airway partially patent for exhalation.
Pharmacology
💊
Medications Reference
RSI agents, paralytics, push-dose pressors
▼
Induction Agents
Ketamine
1–2 mg/kg IV · 4–5 mg/kg IM (DSI/dissociative)
Preferred in hypotension, bronchospasm, or combative patient. Maintains airway reflexes at sub-dissociative dose.
Etomidate
0.3 mg/kg IV
Hemodynamically neutral. Single dose adrenal suppression — avoid in septic shock if alternatives available.
Propofol
1–2 mg/kg IV (reduce in elderly/hypotension)
Caution: significant hypotension. Reserve for normotensive, well-resuscitated patients.
Paralytics
Succinylcholine
1.5 mg/kg IV
Onset ~45–60s, duration ~10 min. Avoid: hyperkalemia, burn >24h, crush injury >24h, denervating disease.
Rocuronium
1.2 mg/kg IV (RSI dose)
Onset ~60s at RSI dose, duration 60–90 min. Reversible with Sugammadex 16 mg/kg.
Push-Dose Pressors (Peri-intubation)
Epinephrine (push-dose)
10–20 mcg IV q2–5min
Dilute: 1 mg/10mL → take 1mL + 9mL NS = 10 mcg/mL. Use for peri-intubation hemodynamic collapse.
Phenylephrine (push-dose)
100–200 mcg IV q5min
Pure alpha — good for tachycardic hypotension. No chronotropy.
Post-Intubation Sedation
Propofol infusion
5–50 mcg/kg/min
Titrate to RASS –2 to –3. Monitor triglycerides if >48h.
Fentanyl infusion
25–200 mcg/hr
Analgesia-first approach. Co-administer with sedation.

BiPAP Options & Settings
Device selection, starting settings, and clinical targets
▼
Available Devices
Servo U Ventilator PREFERRED
Locations: Rooms 15, 16, 27
Modes: BiPAP · CPAP · HFNC · Full MV
Starting IPAP: 10–14 cmH₂O · EPAP: 5–8 cmH₂O
FiO₂: Titrate to SpO₂ ≥94%
Trilogy / Philips BiPAP BACKUP
Starting IPAP: 10 cmH₂O · EPAP: 5 cmH₂O
Titrate IPAP in 2 cmH₂O increments for WOB / SpO₂
Max IPAP typically 20 cmH₂O before escalating
✓
CHF — Pulmonary Edema
CPAP 5–10 cmH₂O or BiPAP IPAP 12/EPAP 5
High FiO₂ initially; titrate down rapidly
✓
COPD Exacerbation
BiPAP IPAP 10–14 / EPAP 4–5
Target SpO₂ 88–92%; avoid over-oxygenation
!
Contraindications
Unable to protect airway · Vomiting · Facial trauma · Hemodynamic instability without correction · Respiratory arrest
