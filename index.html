Airway Decision Framework
🧠
Pre-Intubation Mnemonics
LEMON → HOPPER → SOAP-ME · Assess, optimize, equip, intubate
▼
Real-World ED Flow:
1 · LEMON → 2 · HOPPER → 3 · SOAP-ME → Intubate
LEMON Difficult Airway Prediction — Use this FIRST
L
Look Externally — facial trauma, obesity, beard, short neck
E
Evaluate 3-3-2 Rule — mouth opening (3 fingers), mandible (3 fingers), thyrohyoid distance (2 fingers)
M
Mallampati — Class III/IV predicts difficult view
O
Obstruction — tumor, epiglottitis, blood, foreign body
N
Neck Mobility — C-spine precautions, severe arthritis
🔑 "Will this be a difficult airway?" → Decides RSI vs awake intubation vs backup plan
HOPPER Physiologic Optimization — Right before RSI
H
Hypoxia — pre-oxygenate, apneic O₂, NC at max flow
O
Oxygen Source — confirm O₂ setup and delivery devices
P
Position — ear-to-sternal notch alignment, ramped if obese
P
Pharmacy — induction agent, paralytic, push-dose pressors ready
E
Equipment — laryngoscope, ETT, suction, backup airway confirmed
R
Resuscitation — fluids, pressors, hemodynamics optimized
🔑 "Set the patient up to survive the intubation." — Run aloud as a team before pushing meds
SOAP-ME Equipment Checklist — Setup phase
S
Suction — working Yankauer in hand
O
Oxygen — source confirmed + all delivery devices available
A
Airway Equipment — laryngoscope (direct + video), ETTs, bougie
P
Pharmacy — induction agent + paralytic drawn up
M
Monitors — pulse ox, cardiac, BP, ETCO₂ all active
E
Extra Equipment — bougie, iGel/LMA, surgical airway kit at bedside
🔑 "Do I physically have everything I need?" — Great teaching tool for residents/students

💨 Universal: Nasal Cannula on for ALL pre-oxygenation methods
  • Apply NC at max wall flow during every pre-ox strategy
  • Maintain throughout all laryngoscopy attempts
  • Wall NC at max delivers up to ~75 L/min when measured
  • Provides apneic oxygenation regardless of primary modality
Pre-Oxygenation & Airway Management
🫁
Pre-Oxygenation Standardization
Priority-based hierarchy by clinical scenario
▼
1
BiPAP / CPAP
▸ CHF · Pulmonary edema · COPD · Cooperative patient
First Line — PerIOx Trial
Target SpO₂ ≥95% before laryngoscopy
Servo U (Rooms 15, 16, 27) — supports NIV (BiPAP/CPAP/HFNC) and post-intubation ventilation in a single device
If agitated → consider DSI first, then place BiPAP
NC at max wall flow maintained underneath mask
2
High-Flow Nasal Cannula
▸ BiPAP not tolerated · Spontaneously breathing · Apneic oxygenation during attempts
Second Line
Dedicated HFNC device at 40–60 L/min preferred
Wall NC at max delivers up to ~75 L/min when measured
Leave in place during all laryngoscopy attempts
Servo U supports dedicated HFNC mode
3
NRB + Nasal Cannula
▸ NIPPV not tolerated · Obtunded but breathing
Alternative
NRB at 15 L/min + NC at max wall flow simultaneously
Consider DSI with induction agent of choice → then place BiPAP
NC remains in place during intubation attempts
4
BVM + NC + PEEP Valve
▸ Peri-arrest · Apneic · Pre-arrest respiratory failure
Rescue / Bridge
NC at max wall flow flush under mask (~75 L/min measured)
PEEP valve 5–10 cmH₂O on BVM
Two-person BVM technique preferred
Bridge — escalate to BiPAP or intubation as rapidly as possible

Delayed Sequence Intubation (DSI)
Induction-assisted pre-oxygenation for agitated patients
▼
💡 DSI should be considered standard practice for difficult airways
1
Administer Induction Agent of Choice
Select agent appropriate for clinical scenario — goal is cooperative/sedated state, not apnea
Patient must maintain airway reflexes and respiratory drive
2
Apply Preferred Pre-Ox Modality
Patient now cooperative — place BiPAP or HFNC
NC at max wall flow under mask in all cases
Allow 3–5 minutes of optimization
3
Confirm SpO₂ Optimized
Target ≥95% before proceeding to RSI
4
Proceed with RSI — Paralytic of Choice
Administer paralytic of choice per clinical scenario

Heliox Therapy
Severe obstruction unresponsive to initial treatment
▼
Primary Indications
Status Asthmaticus Severe COPD Exacerbation Upper Airway Obstruction Post-extubation Stridor Croup (severe) Tracheal Stenosis
When to Consider
Severe bronchospasm not responding to initial bronchodilator therapy
Increased work of breathing refractory to standard bronchodilator therapy
Rapidly worsening hypoxia as bridge while escalating treatment
Anatomic upper airway obstruction causing turbulent flow

Aintree Catheter — iGel Technique
Intubation via iGel + Aintree + fiberoptic bronchoscope
▼
Use after denitrogenation with good SpO₂ via iGel — or when direct/video laryngoscopy has failed ×3
1
Place iGel (if not already in)
Size by weight — confirm ventilation and ETCO₂
Attach the included 3-way adapter to iGel — allows bagging through the device during scope insertion and catheter advancement to maintain oxygenation
2
Load Aintree Catheter onto Fiberoptic Bronchoscope
Thread Aintree catheter over fiberoptic bronchoscope ≤5mm OD (purple B-Flex)
Aintree is 2× length of ET tube — prevents introducer loss inside tube
Minimum ET tube size 6.0 (limited by iGel inner diameter)
3
Advance Bronchoscope Through iGel into Trachea
Navigate scope through iGel lumen → glottis → trachea · confirm tracheal rings
4
Advance Aintree Catheter; Remove Scope
Hold Aintree in position · withdraw bronchoscope while keeping catheter still
5
Remove iGel Over Aintree
Can continue bagging through the 15mm adapter until this step if needed
Carefully slide iGel out while maintaining Aintree position in trachea
Do not lose Aintree position!
6
Thread ET Tube Over Aintree into Trachea
Advance ET tube (≥6.0) over Aintree catheter into trachea
Remove Aintree catheter once ET tube is seated
7
Confirm Placement — Fiberoptic + Capnography
Pass fiberoptic bronchoscope through ET tube as second confirmation of tracheal position — confirm tracheal rings and carina on scope
Then confirm with continuous waveform capnography and bilateral breath sounds
⚠ Training: Airway mannequin models available for hands-on review in the admin office. Practice B-Flex + iGel technique before clinical use.

Cricothyrotomy
Surgical airway — CICO situation
▼
Cricothyrotomy technique: A) Identify CTM, B) Stabilize and incise, C) Rotate 90°, D) Dilate, E) Insert tube
🚨 CICO (Cannot Intubate, Cannot Oxygenate) — Declare aloud. Call surgical backup simultaneously.
1
Identify & Mark Cricothyroid Membrane
Extend neck · Palpate thyroid cartilage → inferior notch → CTM soft spot below
Use bedside ultrasound if landmark unclear (obesity, prior surgery)
Mark the neck before sedation/paralytic if time allows
2
Stabilize Larynx — Vertical Skin Incision
Non-dominant hand stabilizes larynx
3–4 cm vertical skin incision over CTM to improve exposure
3
Horizontal Membrane Incision
Horizontal stab incision through CTM in lower third (avoids superior cricothyroid arteries)
Extend with scissors or bougie to widen
4
Insert Airway Device
Hook finger or tracheal hook in incision to hold open · hemostat for blunt dissection if needed
Insert cuffed 6.0 ET tube or dedicated cric tube caudally · inflate cuff
5
Confirm and Secure
Continuous waveform capnography + bilateral breath sounds
Secure tube — convert to formal tracheostomy within 24–72h
Alternative: Melker/Cook Kit (Seldinger Technique)
Kit contains: 18g needle · wire guide · curved dilator · #15 mini scalpel · cuffed catheter (5mm ID / 7.2mm OD)
M1
Identify & Mark CTM · Mini Scalpel Stab
Small skin nick with included #15 mini scalpel over CTM
M2
Needle + Syringe into CTM
18g introducer needle at 45° caudal · aspirate air to confirm tracheal position
M3
Advance Wire Guide
Thread wire guide through needle caudally · remove needle · maintain wire
M4
Dilate Tract · Advance Catheter
Curved dilator over wire to open tract · remove dilator · advance cuffed catheter over wire · remove wire · inflate cuff
M5
Confirm & Secure
Waveform capnography + breath sounds · secure with included tracheostomy tape

Needle Jet Insufflation
Temporary bridge to surgical airway — CICO
▼
A) Airway axis alignment, B) CTM anatomy landmarks, C) Needle insertion technique and anatomy
🚨 Bridge only — not a definitive airway. Risk of fatal barotrauma if expiration is obstructed. Proceed to cricothyrotomy immediately.
1
Identify Cricothyroid Membrane
Extend neck · Palpate thyroid cartilage → CTM soft spot
Ultrasound if landmark unclear
2
Insert 14g Angiocath at 45° Caudal
Aspirate air to confirm tracheal position
Remove needle — advance catheter only
3
Attach High-Pressure Oxygen
Y-connector to wall O₂ at 15 L/min, or dedicated jet ventilator
Alternative: 3 mL syringe barrel + 15mm ET tube adapter → BVM
4
Ventilate: 1 sec IN / 4 sec OUT
Occlude Y-port 1 sec → release 4 sec for passive exhalation
Watch for chest rise; upper airway must be partially patent for exhalation
5
Convert to Definitive Surgical Airway
Bridge <45 min before hypercarbia critical · Escalate to cricothyrotomy or call thoracic surgery immediately
